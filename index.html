2009-01-13
I have to go to the bathroom. Bad. Really bad. If I get up, I'll never get my seat back. Maybe the feeling will subside... With a raging case of undiagnosed ADD or maybe even ADHD, I love FSM. It's always crowded and there's always something to distract me while studying. I can even eat here alone and not feel awkward: plenty of people around to look at, the tables are decorated with newspapers from the '60s, and the CNN effect is always steadily going strong in the background. Magic. Good luck finding a seat, though. I used to cut out of class early in order to find one. And once you do find one, don't even think of getting up until you know you're ready to leave. If you're having trouble, try sharing a table with someone and not so subtly edging them out. The more books you have, the better. Best quality food on campus, but you will pay for it. Go for the soup/salad combo. It's probably the best bang for the buck and the soups are really good. Sometimes they accidentally give you a bowl instead of a cup -- score! Minus one star for not taking credit or ATM cards and only cash or Cal1.

2008-10-23
FSM has some crazy history that I really should know more about, being a bear, but since my knowledge is spotty I refer you to: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Speech_Movement. So let me stick to what I do know: pastries. The pastries here are GOOD, and not unreasonably priced, about $2 each. Some of the yummies I've had here: Chocolate cake, raspberry cheesecake, pumpkin cheesecake, almond croissant, and more. I've been eating this stuff since Freshman year so there's quite a few I'm leaving off, though I do have a penchant for the cheesecakes and chocolate cake. Basically, the pastries are way better than what you could get at the GBC or DCs or the Bear's Lair food court, and what better way is there to get through a class than by eating something yummy for the tummy? I also like the actual Cafe. Cool decor, internet (but of course), and nice, chill atmosphere. Only bad part is the sometimes *insane* lines between classes. Thumbs up for FSM!

2006-10-26
Free Speech is not generally a good place to get laptop work done because the ratio of indoor tables to Berkeley students is ridiculously small and the sun is too bright outdoors. Also, you need to be able to get on the Berkeley network to use wireless, I think, so if you aren't a university affiliate it's no good. The food and coffee are both reasonably good and reasonably priced, although I have learned two important facts about the prepackaged sandwiches that are ubiquitous on Berkeley campus: 1) Roasted vegetables = zucchini 2) Yellow tofu != eggs One day over the summer (fewer students!), I did manage to get a table inside. I guarded it with my life for hours and hours until I was so hungry and so cold and so ... um ... needing-to-use-the-restroom? ... that I finally had to vacate. I pulled out my cell phone to check the time, used the facilities, hurried down to BART, tried to find my cell phone, and discovered that it wasn't in the usual spot in my bag. I suddenly had that terrible sinking feeling you can only have when you realize something that is basically an extension of your brain, like your computer or cell phone or hippocampus, has been lost or destroyed forever. I had left my cell phone in the bathroom. My brain: YOU HAVE TO GET IT BACK Me (to brain): It's probably gone. My brain: WHY WOULD ANYONE TAKE A CELL PHONE THEY ALL HAVE CELL PHONES ALREADY Me (to brain): Hmm. My brain: WE HAVE TO AT LEAST TRY Me (to brain): So we'll go back, and look in the bathroom, but it probably won't be there, so then what? My brain: MAYBE SOMEONE TURNED IT IN Me (to brain): And then we're going to go up to the guy at the cafe and be like Hi uh okay so I'm retarded and left my phone in the bathroom don't ask questions but did anyone like uh find it? My brain: EXACTLY Me (to brain): What if he thinks I was using the vibrate function of my phone for unspeakable purposes? My brain: EW NO ONE DOES THAT EW Me (to brain): Okay well what if he thinks I was calling someone in the bathroom, that is also weird. My brain: IT DOESNT MATTER WHAT HE THINKS IF IT GETS THE PHONE BACK MUST GET PHONE AT ALL COSTS EVEN DEATH SHUT UP AND GO NOW As it turned out, though, all the worrying about how awkward it would be to try to explain that I had left a phone in a bathroom was for naught because it was still on top of the toilet paper dispenser. Thus, at least one of two things is true: 1) Berkeley students are not thieves or 2) Things left in bathrooms are too potentially gross to steal. I haven't tried this experiment somewhere other than Berkeley to see which is the case, but maybe someday.
